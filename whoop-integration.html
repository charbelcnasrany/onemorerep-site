RepCall × WHOOP — Integration UX

Prepared for WHOOP app review. RepCall is a social fitness app: you prove you worked out (location-verified, optional photo), a small circle of friends sees it, and streaks + friendly nudges keep everyone showing up. Connecting WHOOP is optional and lets a member attach their real workout to a check-in.

Step 1 · Connect WHOOP (Settings)

In Settings, the member taps Connect WHOOP. The standard WHOOP OAuth screen opens; scopes requested are read:workout, read:profile, and offline.

Step 2 · Attach your workout to a check-in

After checking in, the member taps Add workout stats → Pull from WHOOP. We fetch their most recent WHOOP workout and read its active calories and day strain. Numbers are pulled straight from WHOOP, never typed in, so they can't be faked.

Step 3 · It shows on the friend feed

The pulled stats appear as a quiet "This session" strip on the check-in, with a WHOOP source chip. This is the visible footprint of WHOOP data in the app: a member's own session stats on their own post, seen only by friends they've added.

Step 4 · A real workout can verify a check-in

A check-in away from a recognised venue posts as Unverified. If the member's connected WHOOP workout clears a simple effort bar (≥ 10 day strain or ≥ 500 active calories), that same-day check-in is upgraded to Verified. WHOOP confirms genuine effort; it never grants status over other users.

Privacy & security

WHOOP tokens live exclusively server-side (never on-device) · a member's WHOOP stats are visible only to friends they explicitly added · WHOOP data is never sold or shared for advertising · disconnecting WHOOP or deleting your RepCall account deletes the data · full detail in our Privacy Policy.
